# Shopify Client Outreach Email Sequence

**For:** Safeer — Freelance Shopify + n8n + AI Agent Developer  
**Positioning:** Premium ($150–300/hr), white-label friendly  
**Target:** Shopify store owners (DTC brands, premium positioning)  
**Sequence:** 5 emails over 21 days  

---

## Prospect Research Methodology

**How to find stores to target:**
1. **Shopify Exchange** — Browse sold stores to see what stacks/automation exist
2. **BuiltWith.com** — Filter for Hydrogen/Dawn stores, filter by traffic tier
3. **LinkedIn** — Search "Shopify Developer" + "Hydrogen" to find store owners
4. **Comp Thrul** — Spy on competitor tech stacks
5. **Niche communities** — Indie Makers, DTC brands, r/Shopify

**Qualifying criteria:**
- $50k+/mo revenue (indicates they can afford premium)
- 3+ employees (has operational pain)
- 10+ products (meaningful catalog)
- Using 5+ apps (bloat = automation opportunity)

**Research checklist per prospect:**
- [ ] Store theme (Hydrogen/Dawn/custom)
- [ ] Top 3 apps installed
- [ ] Recent blog posts / social activity
- [ ] Any press/reviews
- [ ] Product categories and pricing
- [ ] Traffic indicators (SimilarWeb if public)

---

## Setup Notes

- **Personalization slots** are marked with `[BRACKETS]` — fill these per prospect
- **Timing:** Day 0 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 12 → Day 21
- **Subject lines:** A/B test 2–3 variants per email
- **Sender:** Use a proper domain (not @gmail), ideally with your name and LinkedIn
- **Sign-off:** Keep it human — no "Best regards" corporate filler

---

## Email 1 — The Observation (Day 0)

**Purpose:** Hook with specificity. Zero ask beyond "curious if this resonates."

**Subject variants:**
- `Quick question about [store name]`
- `Your [theme name] store — one observation`
- `Saw your Hydrogen setup, one thing`

**Body:**

```
Hi [first name],

I came across [store name] while researching premium DTC brands 
using [their theme: Hydrogen/Dawn] — [specific thing you noticed: 
e.g., "your collection pages are well-curated" or "the brand 
aesthetic really comes through"].

Most brands on [their stack] hit a wall when they want to add 
[custom section / automation / speed optimization] without 
bloating load times or breaking what's already working.

Curious if that's shown up for you.

— [Your name]
[LinkedIn URL if relevant]
```

**Notes:** 4–6 sentences max. The personalization is the hook — make it specific to THEIR store, not generic Shopify stats.

---

## Email 2 — The Pattern (Day 3)

**Purpose:** Follow up with a different angle. Offer value first.

**Subject variants:**
- `Re: Quick question about [store name]`
- `Following up — [their brand]`
- `[First name], one more thing`

**Body:**

```
Hi [first name],

Didn't hear back — wanted to share one more thing.

[Store name] is in the [their niche: e.g., sustainable apparel, 
home goods] space, right? Brands in that lane typically struggle 
with [their biggest pain point: manual inventory sync / Klaviyo 
setup / multi-channel reporting].

The thing is — most of those problems are solvable in a 2-week 
audit without touching the core setup. I've done this for 
[similar brand] and [specific outcome: "cut their order processing 
time by 40%" / "got their Core Web Vitals from 54 to 91"].

Happy to share more if it's useful.

— [Your name]
```

**Notes:** Reference something industry-specific. Show you understand their world, not just Shopify generically.

---

## Email 3 — The Pivot (Day 7)

**Purpose:** Change the angle entirely. Different pain point, different hook.

**Subject variants:**
- `Your [their stack] — operational question`
- `One different angle on [store name]`
- `Automation + Shopify — question`

**Body:**

```
Hi [first name],

I know — I'm that person who keeps showing up in your inbox. 
Last one I promise.

Here's the thing I've been thinking about for brands like yours:

[Shopify native reporting is limited for multi-channel brands] / 
[Manual order processing eats hours every week] / [App bloat 
slows stores down] — pick the angle you didn't address in Email 1.

For [similar brand], we solved it by [what you did: building a 
custom n8n workflow / optimizing their Dawn theme / adding 
AI-powered product descriptions]. Took [timeframe], cost around 
[rough ballpark or "a fraction of what a full-time hire would"].

If you've already got this handled, ignore me. If not — what's 
the biggest operational time sink right now?

— [Your name]
```

**Notes:** Paradox of saying "last one" + still offering value. The question at the end invites a reply.

---

## Email 4 — The Breakup Email (Day 12)

**Purpose:** Paradoxically increases reply rates. People respond to be polite or satisfy curiosity.

**Subject variants:**
- `I'll take the hint — [store name]`
- `Last email from me`
- `Going quiet on [store name]`

**Body:**

```
Hi [first name],

I don't want to clutter your inbox.

If timing's not right for a conversation about [their main pain 
point], just say so and I'll leave you alone. No hard feelings.

If you ever want to chat shop about [their niche] or share what 
you're working on operationally, I'm around.

Either way — good luck with [their store / upcoming launch / 
season].

— [Your name]
```

**Notes:** This email often gets replies. Either "no, please keep me in mind" or "actually, let's talk." The low-pressure close is key.

---

## Email 5 — The Re-engagement (Day 21–30)

**Purpose:** Final touch. Leave them with something useful even if they never reply. Sets up future relationship.

**Subject variants:**
- `[Store name] — one resource before I go`
- `Last one — a useful read for [their niche]`
- `Quick resource for Shopify brands like yours`

**Body:**

```
Hi [first name],

One last thing from me — I promise.

I put together a short guide on [topic relevant to their pain 
point: "common Hydrogen performance pitfalls" / "automation 
workflows that pay for themselves" / "questions to ask any 
Shopify developer before hiring"].

[Link to resource / or offer to send directly]

No strings. Just thought it might be useful.

Hope [something relevant: "your Q2 launch goes well" / 
"your new collection gets great traction"].

— [Your name]
```

**Notes:** This is a "soft" final email. You're leaving value behind, not asking for anything. Future-oriented.
